## 1. Problem Statement
Consumers who want to avoid toxic or harmful ingredients in home and personal care products face a research process that is slow, fragmented, and requires expertise most people don't have. Product labels are unreliable — "clean" and "natural" claims are unverified — and there is no single place to apply accumulated preferences across all future purchases.

## 2. ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
A consumer who is actively replacing products in their home — cleaning supplies, personal care, pantry staples — with cleaner, non-toxic alternatives. They already care about ingredient safety but lack the chemistry background to interpret labels quickly. They've likely used EWG's Skin Deep or similar resources before, but find jumping between sources and starting from scratch each time unsustainable. They are buying for a household, often a family, and want to build up a trusted set of brands and preferences over time rather than re-researching the same ground.

## 3. Pain Points
- Ingredient lists require expertise to interpret; most consumers can't evaluate what they're reading on a label.
- "Clean," "natural," and "non-toxic" marketing claims are unverified and routinely mislead shoppers.
- Research is fragmented across product sites, ingredient databases, and review sources — high effort for each new purchase.
- Preferences and trusted brands built up through past research have no persistent home — users start over each time.

## 4. Proposed Solution
Clean Shopper is an AI-powered research assistant with a persistent preference layer. A user can:

- Describe what they're looking for and receive product recommendations with ingredient safety assessments and reasoning.
- Compare two or more products side by side to get a clear, preference-aware recommendation.
- Save ingredients to avoid and brands or certifications to favor (EWG Verified, USDA Organic, B Corp, etc.), which are automatically applied to all future recommendations.
- Add recommended products to a shopping cart that persists across sessions.
- Organize saved products into a personal library by category.

## 6. Design Constraints
**Platform:** Web (React/Vite, deployed to Vercel)
**Geography:** Not defined in source material
**Accessibility:** Not defined in source material
**Technical:**
- Frontend: React (Vite) with Tailwind CSS only — no other CSS approach
- State: React useState and useContext; no Redux or Zustand
- Database: Supabase (PostgreSQL) for persistence; local state for session state
- AI: Claude API, model pinned to `claude-sonnet-4-20250514` — no other model permitted
- Ingredient data: EWG Skin Deep API
- Deployment: Vercel
- Single-user, no authentication in V1
